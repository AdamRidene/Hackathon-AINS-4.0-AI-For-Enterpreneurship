# Jury Review — "Firasa" (فِراسة)

**Reviewer:** Lead jury member, AINS Hackathon 2026 — *AI for Entrepreneurship*
**Subject:** Intelligent Entrepreneurial Orientation Engine submission
**Verdict in one line:** A genuinely strong, top-tier submission that meets essentially every MUST criterion — but two correctness bugs quietly undercut its own headline differentiator, and the evaluation is more self-confirming than it looks. Fixing the items below would move it from "very good" to "winning."

Verified during review: `pytest` → **18/18 pass**; `eval_protocol` → diagnostic Top-1 **1.00**, RAG mean P@5 **0.96**, scoring-consistency **pass**; KB = **32** real bilingual Tunisian resources. The claims in the README reproduce.

---

## What is genuinely good (so the team gets credit where due)

- **Real integration, not three panels.** One shared `ProjectProfile`, one `run_audit()` orchestrator; the diagnostic feeds the gap detector, P_coh feeds the commercial score, and gaps *and* gated scores both drive RAG retrieval. This is exactly what the brief said it would evaluate ("genuinely interact, or merely coexist").
- **Rule-based classifier with a per-gate evidence trace.** Satisfies "every stage assignment links to specific collected data points" — a property an LLM could not guarantee. The LLM is correctly confined to secondary duties with deterministic fallbacks, so the pipeline runs end-to-end with no model installed.
- **Explainable scoring.** Every score carries a per-criterion contribution trace, base-vs-gated values, and `missing_inputs`. The gate model (cap market at 30 without validation; halve scalability when D_man>7) is a defensible "weak fundamental can't be masked" mechanism.
- **Anti-hallucination retrieval.** The routing matrix filters the corpus to eligible institutions *before* cosine ranking, so a legal-form gap structurally cannot surface a green-energy program. KB exceeds the 30-resource bar with real, bilingual entries.
- **Honesty.** `SCORING_METHODOLOGY.md` documents two deliberate deviations from the concept note and even flags an internal inconsistency in the note's own worked example. That intellectual honesty is a plus.

## Findings, by severity

Severity = impact on the brief's acceptance criteria and judging dimensions (Real-world Impact 25 / Technical Depth 25 / Prototype Quality 20 / Explainability & Scoring 15 / Evaluation 15).

### 🔴 CRITICAL — these attack the core differentiator

**C1. Objective classification is capped by the founder's self-declaration — which defeats the whole "evidence over self-perception" thesis.**
The two evidence tokens needed to reach stages 4–6 (`months_unit_economics`, `repeatable_sales`) are only *asked* when the founder declares Fundraising+ or Growth (`applies=lambda p: _advanced_claim(p)` / `== GROWTH`). So a founder who is objectively Growth-ready but humbly declares "Ideation" is never asked for them, and the classifier caps out at Structuration.

*Proven during review:* a profile with Production MVP + revenue model + SARL + D_man=2 + validation, declaring "Ideation," classifies as **stage 3 (Structuration)** — its `months_unit_economics` is `None`. Underestimation can therefore **never be detected past stage 3**, the blockers/roadmap mis-orient an advanced founder toward early-stage resources, and the celebrated perception–reality gap becomes partly circular: *you can only be classified as high as you dared to claim.*
**Fix:** branch the intake on sector and on prior factual answers — never on the self-assessment you are trying to audit. Always collect the gate-4/5/6 evidence probes. Re-ordering questions adaptively is fine; withholding an evidence token the classifier depends on is not.

**C2. The most mature founders feed the *wrong text* to the value-proposition judge.**
`validation_proof` and `vp_narrative` both write to `commercial.value_proposition_narrative`, and for Fundraising+ claims only `validation_proof` is served (`vp_narrative.applies = not _advanced_claim`).

*Proven during review:* an advanced founder's stored narrative is `"We hold 40 signed LOIs worth 200k TND from pilot clients."` — a validation statement, not a value proposition. The P_coh judge scores VP coherence on problem clarity / segment / differentiation, none of which that text contains, so the **Commercial score is artificially depressed for exactly the most advanced founders.**
**Fix:** give validation proof its own field; keep the VP narrative separate; ask both.

### 🟠 MEDIUM — rigor and methodology

**M3. The evaluation proves *consistency*, not *accuracy*.** Diagnostic ground truth for 6 of 10 cases is "a profile built to satisfy gates 1..k" — labels generated by the very logic under test, so Top-1 = 1.00 is tautological. The remaining 4 are author-labelled (n=4). For RAG, candidates are hard-filtered to the gap category *before* ranking and the "relevant" set ≈ "the institutions in that category," so P@5 ≈ 0.96 mostly confirms the routing filter rather than ranking quality (tellingly, `missing_legal_form` scored 0.8 only because the hand-written relevant set omitted *Startup Act*, which is in fact correctly routed). The README is upfront about this, but a jury reads it as weak external validity.
**Fix:** add a small *independent* labelled set (real or expert-labelled profiles with human-assigned stages) and report accuracy there; for RAG add human relevance judgments and a ranking metric (nDCG / MRR) plus negative controls.

**M4. Three of the five scores can be maxed by confident self-reporting.** Innovation, Green, and much of Commercial are pure functions of self-declared categoricals — claim "Global" novelty + list five tech words + "Registered" IP → Innovation ≈ 100 with zero evidence; list many SDGs → Green inflates (`count/17`). Only Market has an evidence gate. The brief stresses "structural intelligence beyond retrieval"; here the anomaly pass catches a few cross-field contradictions but nothing for tech-hype or SDG-stuffing.
**Fix:** add gates / cross-checks to the other dimensions (e.g., require an evidence token for "Registered" IP; cap SDG credit; flag novelty inconsistent with competitor count), and surface a per-score confidence reflecting evidenced-vs-declared inputs.

**M5. The roadmap's "time horizons" are metadata pass-through, not reasoning.** `Milestone.horizon = fresh[0].horizon` — the horizon is a property of whichever KB chunk was retrieved, so two different founders get the same horizon for the same resource. The brief insists "a roadmap has an order, a rationale, and time horizons." Order and rationale are real; the horizon is not derived from the founder's situation. (The secondary horizon sort in `build_roadmap` is also dead code — `m.order` is already unique.)
**Fix:** derive horizon from blocker dependency / stage-distance (next blocking gate = immediate, etc.) and sequence by dependency.

**M6. Routing mismatch for growth/operations blockers.** `GATE_DOMAIN[6] = "organisational"` → `DOMAIN_TO_GAP` → `premature_fundraising` → financing institutions. A scaling blocker (repeatable sales, human dependency) is routed to *financing* resources — the wrong remedy. The `_meta` also advertises a `scalability` gap category that has **zero** chunks (routing silently redirects it).
**Fix:** add scaling/operations resources, route organisational blockers to them, and either populate or remove the empty `scalability` category.

### 🟡 LOWER — prototype quality, privacy, scope discipline

**L7. Bilingual support is skin-deep exactly where it matters most.** The intake renders `prompt_ar` and the KB is bilingual, but the entire **audit dashboard is hardcoded French** (Dashboard receives no `lang` prop; GapBanner / ScorePanel / Roadmap / Explainability are FR-only), and the assistant's prompt hardcodes "Réponds en français." An Arabic user gets an Arabic questionnaire, then a French result and a French chat. Either commit to full FR/AR on the deliverable surface or scope the "Arabic" bonus honestly.

**L8. A fake SaaS paywall distracts from — and slightly contradicts — the mission.** `App.jsx` limits a Tunisian entrepreneur to 1 project on a "free" plan with an upgrade modal (all `localStorage`, nothing real). For a PNUD/GEWEET inclusion-focused brief, an invented monetization wall on the primary user reads as scope creep and is a credibility risk in the live demo. Remove it or hide it for judging.

**L9. The Privacy & Security NFR is barely addressed.** `redact()` masks only the VP narrative on one GET; full audits (names, sectors, scores) are stored in SQLite and `GET /api/projects` returns *all* of them; there is no auth (the frontend "login" is `localStorage`); CORS is `allow_origins=["*"]`. The brief explicitly asks what sensitive data is used and how it is protected, and there is no privacy note.
**Fix:** add a short data-handling statement, scope access per project, drop the global list endpoint (or auth-gate it).

**L10. "Mon Parcours" is fragmented and device-bound.** "Actions taken" (checked milestones) and the score-vector history live in browser `localStorage`, while audits live server-side in SQLite. Switching device loses progress, and there is no single server-backed tracking timeline as the brief describes.
**Fix:** persist milestone completion and parcours events server-side keyed to `project_id`.

**L11. Smaller items.** Dead `self_assessment.declared_revenue` / `declared_legal_form` fields (set in seed scenarios, never asked, never read by the gap detector). `_rank_blockers` labels *every* unmet gate `structural_gap` once `intake_complete` — so the C1 missing-unit-economics gate is reported as a structural gap rather than "missing information." `confidence` is a generic completeness proxy, not classification certainty. KB URLs are self-flagged "re-verify before production."

## Priority order for the team (highest leverage first)

1. **C1** — stop gating evidence collection on self-declaration (this is the difference between a real audit engine and a sophisticated form).
2. **C2** — separate validation-proof from the VP narrative (one-field fix, removes an unfair scoring penalty).
3. **M3** — add a genuinely independent labelled test, however small; it converts "Evaluation & Rigour" from defensible to convincing.
4. **M4 / M5 / M6** — broaden gates beyond Market, make horizons reasoned, fix growth-blocker routing.
5. **L8 / L7 / L9** — remove the paywall for the demo, finish or honestly scope Arabic, add a privacy note.

Net: the architecture and explainability are already at winning level. The risk to this team is not ambition — it is that **C1 and C2 silently weaken the exact feature they are pitching as the differentiator**, and a sharp judge will find them in the live demo. Fix those two and tighten the evaluation, and this is a front-runner.
